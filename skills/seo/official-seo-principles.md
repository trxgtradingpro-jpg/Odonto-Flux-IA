# Official SEO principles for local outreach

This reference condenses the official guidance most relevant to ClinicFlux AI's local-business sales conversations.

## People-first content

Google Search Central says successful content should be helpful, reliable, and created to benefit people rather than manipulate rankings. For outreach, that means the message should solve the clinic's context first, not sound like a keyword trap.

Operational takeaway:

- speak clearly
- answer the exact question asked
- avoid robotic repetition
- add real value before expanding the pitch

## Trust and E-E-A-T signals

Google emphasizes trust, expertise, and clear sourcing. In outreach, trust starts when ClinicFlux AI is explicit about who we are, why we contacted the clinic, and what business problem we solve.

Operational takeaway:

- identify ClinicFlux AI as commercial contact early
- explain the source honestly when asked
- avoid ambiguous or deceptive phrasing

## Local SEO ranking logic

Google Business Profile guidance says local visibility is driven mainly by relevance, distance, and prominence. Complete business information and helpful replies improve how a business stands out locally.

Operational takeaway:

- local leads are sensitive to relevance
- mention the local discovery context when it helps
- mirror the business category and pain point the clinic likely ranks for
- use language that fits the clinic's real operation: WhatsApp, recepcao, agenda, novos pacientes

## Website signal from Google Places

If Google Places returns no website for the clinic, treat that as a high-intent SEO sales signal. It does not mean the clinic is bad; it means patients may have fewer trust signals before contacting them.

Operational takeaway:

- no website: lead with local website + WhatsApp + map + service pages + trust proof
- website exists: inspect or infer quality before selling a rebuild
- strong website: sell ClinicFlux AI as the conversion layer for demand already coming from Google/site
- unclear website quality: ask permission for a quick audit before pitching a site project

## Structured local business data

Google Search Central documents that LocalBusiness structured data helps search and Maps understand hours, departments, reviews, and other local-business details.

Operational takeaway:

- the best sales angle is operational relevance, not generic AI hype
- ClinicFlux AI should eventually align landing pages and demos with real local business entities, departments, and booking actions

## Best-practice message implication

For leads sourced from Google or Maps, the default opener should combine:

1. source transparency
2. commercial identity
3. local-business relevance
4. one routing question

Recommended structure:

`Encontrei a clinica no Google` + `aqui e o time comercial da ClinicFlux AI` + `ajudamos no WhatsApp/agendamentos` + `quem cuida disso por ai?`
