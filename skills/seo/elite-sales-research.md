# Elite sales research for SaaS and website offers

This reference captures the research pattern behind the 90/100 local SEO sales skill.

## Research places used

- Google Search Central: people-first content, E-E-A-T, and SEO as a way to help users and search engines.
- Google Business Profile Help: local ranking depends mainly on relevance, distance, and prominence; complete information, reviews, photos, and helpful replies matter.
- Google Search Central LocalBusiness data: local pages can expose hours, departments, reviews, and booking/action context.
- HubSpot SaaS landing page examples: strong SaaS pages lead with clear value, product proof, demo/free trial CTAs, and concrete outcomes.
- TechRadar website builder comparison plus SaaS market examples: website builders sell speed, ease, design, AI, SEO basics, ecommerce, and all-in-one simplicity.

## 10 SaaS patterns to study

The exact "top 10" can change, but these products are useful pattern references for selling ClinicFlux AI:

- Salesforce: sells CRM as revenue control, not just software.
- Microsoft Cloud: sells integration and reliability.
- Adobe: sells creative output with less busywork.
- ServiceNow: sells operational workflow control.
- HubSpot: sells marketing, sales, CRM, and automation in one ecosystem.
- Shopify: sells commerce outcomes and conversion infrastructure.
- Zoom: sells familiar communication plus AI productivity.
- Asana: sells work visibility and execution.
- Dropbox: sells simple storage and collaboration clarity.
- Notion: sells one workspace for docs, projects, knowledge, and AI.

Reusable SaaS lesson:

- lead with outcome
- show the workflow
- reduce time-to-value
- make proof visible
- use one CTA: demo, trial, audit, or quick call

## 10 website builder and website seller patterns to study

Use these as market references when deciding how to sell a site to a clinic:

- Wix: quick all-in-one site for small businesses.
- Hostinger: low-cost speed and AI site creation.
- Shopify: ecommerce and checkout conversion.
- Squarespace: polished design and brand trust.
- Weebly/Square: simple local business web presence.
- HubSpot CMS: website tied to CRM and marketing.
- GoDaddy: domain, hosting, site, and business tools in one place.
- WordPress: flexible SEO/content foundation.
- Webflow: design control, CMS, and performance-oriented builds.
- Framer: fast modern marketing sites with strong visual polish.

Reusable website-sales lesson:

- no website: sell trust and local discoverability first
- weak website: sell audit and conversion gaps first
- existing strong website: sell SaaS as the next conversion layer
- never sell "a pretty site" alone; sell patient trust, WhatsApp clicks, service pages, map presence, speed, and measurement

## ClinicFlux AI offer routing

Use this order:

1. If no website exists, offer local website/SEO first.
2. If the website exists but lacks booking, WhatsApp CTA, service pages, map, or trust proof, offer a quick website/SEO audit.
3. If the website exists and the WhatsApp flow is active, offer ClinicFlux AI SaaS as the response and scheduling layer.
4. If the clinic is already engaged with a responsible person, offer a demo.
5. If the clinic declines, stop and leave a respectful closing.

## Humanized sales timing

The best sales moment is usually after one of these signals:

- the clinic asks how we found them
- the clinic asks what we searched
- reception confirms it handles agendamentos
- a decision maker or responsible phone is shared
- Google Places shows no website
- the clinic's autoresponder reveals slow WhatsApp response, opening hours, forms, or manual triage

Do not pitch before answering the signal. Answer first, then bridge.
